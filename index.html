My Story and Passions

I'm Dhairya, a high school student with a deep interest in what makes people act. This journey into Neuroeconomics began when I realized the complex systems of the brain guide our behavior in the market and in daily life, often in surprising ways.

Beyond academic exploration, my focus sharpens on the tennis court. The need for quick, high-pressure decisions in competitive tennis is a practical lesson in fast thinking and efficiency—a mirror to the quick decision systems I study in neuroscience.

This website serves as my ongoing portfolio, showing my commitment to learning and exploration. I believe that understanding the brain is the key to mastering behavior, whether it's customer choice or personal growth.

Core Research Principles

  • Decision-making is driven by System 1 (fast/intuitive) thinking.
  • Behavior is often governed by anticipating rewards (dopamine loops).
  • Losses are psychologically twice as powerful as equivalent gains.
  • Attention is captured by visual saliency (color, placement, novelty).
